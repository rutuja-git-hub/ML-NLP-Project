Natural Language Processing (NLP) is the ability of a computer program to understand human language as it is spoken and written. The best-known example of NLP, smart assistants such as Siri and Alexa have become increasingly integrated into our lives. Using NLP, they break language down into parts of speech, word stems and other linguistic features

Natural language inference (NLI) is the most common approach to fine-tuning a generic monolingual sentence transformer. The original SBERT “bert-base-nli-mean-tokens” was trained with this, which is one of the highest performing models.

The model that we used, called "bert-base-nli-mean-tokens" is a sentence-transformers model that maps sentences & paragraphs to a 768 dimensional dense vector space and can be used for tasks like clustering or semantic search as seen above.

Our Model's Performance

In the masked language modeling (MLM) portion of the training process, we observed the loss value decreasing as the model was trained further, indicating that the model was learning to correctly predict masked terms over time.

In the semantic textual similarity (STS) portion of the training process, we observed the cosine pearson correlation value increasing as the model was trained further, indicating that the model was more accurately predicting the similarity between two sentences with further training.
